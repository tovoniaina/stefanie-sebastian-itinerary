Itinerary

Day 1 ⇒ Arrival in Antananarivo (Tana in short then transfer to the Hotel)

Arrive in Antananarivo, the capital of Madagascar. After landing, transfer to your hotel to rest and prepare for your upcoming adventure. Enjoy the warm atmosphere and Malagasy hospitality upon arrival.

Overnight : Chalet des Roses Hotel

Day 2 ⇒ Tana - Andasibe (Vakona Reserve visit + Night walk visit)

Depart from Tana heading to Andasibe. Upon arrival, visit the Vakona Reserve, where you will have the opportunity to see lemurs up close, along with crocodiles and other endemic species. The reserve also offers activities such as canoeing on the lake surrounded by forest.

at dusk, we'll be setting off on a nocturnal tour in search of lemurs, and many more…

Overnight : Andasibe Cyperus Hotel

Day 3 ⇒ Drive from Andasibe to Palmarium (Ankanin’ny Nofy) then take a fast boat

Leave Andasibe for Palmarium via a scenic route. Upon arrival in Manambato, take a fast boat to reach Ankanin’ny Nofy, a true haven of peace. Enjoy the serene surroundings and beautiful landscapes.

Overnight : Palmarium Lodge

Day 4 ⇒ Palmarium (Day + night walk visit in Palmarium reserve & Relax on the beautiful, quiet beach)

Palmarium has a reserve where some species of lemurs are found there. This is the best place to see the aye-aye and other species of animals. Beside, Palmarium is a haven for relaxation, with its pretty seaside and lush green vegetation. You will surely enjoy the visit walking through the forest while accompanied by your local guide who will help you spot as many animals as possible. A group of indri, sifakas, red lemurs, black lemurs, crowned lemurs and black and white ruffed lemurs can be observed easily.

Overnight : Palmarium Lodge

Day 5 ⇒ Palmarium - Andasibe (Maromizaha reserve visit) then Analamazaotra national parc visit

In the morning, return to Andasibe and visit the Maromizaha Reserve, where you can spot the Indri Indri, the largest lemur species. In the afternoon, explore the Alanamazaotra Reserve, rich in biodiversity and stunning landscapes.

Overnight : Andasibe Cyperus Hotel

Day 6 ⇒ Andasibe (Mantadia National Park visit)

Spend the day visiting Mantadia National Park, known for its hiking trails through dense forest and picturesque waterfalls. In the evening.

Overnight : Andasibe Cyperus Hotel

Day 7 ⇒ Andasibe - Antsirabe (Have a lunch in Tana then off to Antsirabe)

Return to Tana, upon arrival have a lunch. Afterwards, We leave Tana for Antsirabe, nicknamed the city of water, where you will spend the night.

Overnight: Royal Palace Hotel

Day 8 ⇒ Antsirabe - Ranomafana (National Park)

Your adventure continues towards Ranomafana National Park, renowned for its diverse flora and fauna, including the bamboo lemur and the striped-tail lemur. You'll explore trails through the tropical rainforest and discover the incredible biodiversity of the region. You will spend the night at Setam Lodge.

Overnight : Setam Lodge

Day 9 ⇒ Ranomafana - Fianarantsoa

After a morning of exploration in Ranomafana National parc, you'll head to Fianarantsoa, a historic town surrounded by stunning mountain landscapes. You'll stay at Zomatel Hotel.

Overnight : Zomatel

Day 10 ⇒ Fianarantsoa - Ambalavao - Anja Parc - Isalo

This morning, we leave Fianarantsoa heading southwards to Ambalavao. We visit a workshop making paper fabric and then the zebu market to see locals doing their deals. And then, we continue to Anja reserve home of the ring-tail lemurs. We visit this reserve and then after lunch, we descend into the west coast region leading to Isalo where the scenery changes to open savannah grasslands with zebu herds.

Overnight: Hotel H1

Day 11 ⇒ Isalo National parc Visit

We start the hike following the trail that unveils impressive canyons. The trekking leads to the famous “La Piscine Naturelle”, a natural swimming pool fed by crystal clear streams. You can spend few moments to refresh (do not forget your swimming gear in case tempted). Then, we continue our hike following the “Namaza trail”, leading to the camping site where three species of lemurs (the browns, the ring-tails and the dancing sifakas) drop by expecting food and come for drink.

Overnight: Hotel H1

Day 12 ⇒ Isalo - Toliara - Ifaty

After breakfast, we drive to Tuléar, the capital of the Vezo fishing people. On our way, we will cross one the numerous villages formed by the sapphire rush. Besides, the famous Mahafaly tombs abound all along the way between Sakaraha and Tuléar, having distinct cultural traits of this typical southern tribe, whose tombs display images that represent the daily life or dreams of the deceased. We shall have lunch in Tuléar and then continue driving to Ifaty.

Overnight: Nautilus Hotel

Day 13 ⇒ Ifaty (Beach Resort)

You have full day at leisure during your whole stay in Ifaty. However, some activities can be arranged by your hotel, on your charge. Ifaty grants you a relaxing place by the beach where you can go diving, snorkeling, taking a dugout canoe ride with the Vezo fishermen. Besides, Ifaty spiny forest abounded by extraordinary vegetation of euphorbia, didieracea, and aloes is another important place to see the giant baobabs, the nocturnal southern sportive lemurs, some endemic birds like the Sub-desert mesite, the Running coua found nowhere else.

Overnight: Nautilus Hotel

Day 14 ⇒ Ifaty - Toliara - Tana (1h30min of flight)

Depart back to Tuléar and then transfer to the airport for flight back to Tana. And then, upon arrival, you will be transferred to your hotel.

Overnight: Chalet des Roses Hotel

Day 15 ⇒ City Tour + Transfer to the airport

This morning, we shall have an ample time for city tour visiting some places, Analakely Market, the lake Anosy and the famous Digue handicraft market for souvenirs made from raffia, woodcarvings, semi-precious stones, and so forth. Then, drive to the airport for flight taking you back home.

Money and credit cards

Understanding Money in Madagascar:

  • Currency: The Malagasy currency is the Ariary (MGA), divided into 5 Iraimbilanja.
  • Availability: Ariary is primarily available at local banks, official exchange offices in hotels, and the airport in Antananarivo.
  • Recommendation: We suggest bringing cash in Euros (EUR), British Pounds (GBP), or US Dollars (USD) for exchange. Tip: Exchanging a small amount (200-350 EUR) for Ariary upon arrival at the airport is convenient. You can exchange more throughout your trip as needed. Remember, leftover Ariary cannot be converted back to foreign currency, so exchange smaller amounts at a time. Important: Avoid black markets for currency exchange.

Credit Cards:

  • Limited Use: Credit cards are only accepted in some hotels, primarily in major cities like the capital (Antananarivo) and provincial capitals.
  • Fees Apply: Using your credit card may incur fees depending on your bank.
  • ATMs: Withdrawing cash from ATMs is possible at major banks, but ATMs are not widely available, especially in rural areas.

Bottom Line: Cash is king in Madagascar, especially outside major cities. Plan to use cash for most purchases.

Visa Requirements

For stays in Madagascar, a short-term visa is required. Here's a breakdown of the cost:

  • Less than 15 days: €10 (administrative fee)
  • More than 15 days: €35 (visa fee)

Obtaining Your Visa:

There are two convenient ways to secure your visa:

  1. Apply Online: This is the recommended option to avoid any potential delays upon arrival. Visit the official government website:  https://evisamada-mg.com/en/home
  1. Visa on Arrival: Obtain your visa upon arrival at the airport immigration office by presenting your passport.

Tips and gratuities

We believe tipping is a personal choice for each traveler. However, it's important to understand that tipping is customary in Madagascar, where wages tend to be lower. Consider tipping to show your appreciation for good service.

Here's a general guideline:

  • Drivers: €5 USD per day
  • Local Guides: €3-€5 USD per day
  • National Guides: $8-$10 USD per day
  • Restaurant Waiters: 10% of the bill (adjust based on service quality)

Explanation:

  • Drivers: Drivers typically receive a smaller tip compared to guides.
  • Local Guides: Local guides, who might be specific to a town or region, can be tipped in this range.
  • National Guides: National guides, who are often more experienced and have broader knowledge, can receive a slightly higher tip.
